A Book a Week


As we are all in quarantine, it is a great opportunity to use our spare time
to catch up on all the books that we have wanted to read. For the summer,
I plan to read a book a week.


Week 1: Encounters from Africa. P. Williams. 2000

Eventhough this book was written two decades ago, the topics raised and discussed are still relevant in Africa.
The book holds very interesting short stories from all over Africa. A major theme in most of the stories were reflections
on the social, economic, and cultural interaction Africans have in their communities.
Reading this book, I was able to get a better understand of what people in other African countries go through.
I also got to see the similarities that Africans have despite the large cultural differences.
I believe this book is a beautiful and successful compilation of stories written about Africa by Africans.



Week 2: Anthills of the Savannah

Anthill's of the Savannah narrates the fictitious land of Kangan and it's capital city Bassa, which is marked by social unrest and political intrigue.
Through the narration, Achebe aimed to show that traditional tribal beliefs have a place in African modernity in postcolonial times.
Although it is well crafted, Achebe's fifth book found it hard to follow the momentum gained by the novel giants of the preceding first four books.
